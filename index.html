in Real-World Scenarios: A Dataset and Benchmark
Valfride Nascimento1 Rayson Laroca1,2 Gabriel Lima1 David Menotti1
1Federal University of Paraná

Abstract

While significant advancements have been made in super-resolution for License Plate Recognition (LPR) to address the challenges posed by low-resolution (LR) and degraded images in surveillance, traffic monitoring, and forensic applications, many recent studies still rely on private datasets or simplified degradation models, where LR images are often generated by downsampling high-resolution (HR) images. To address this issue, we introduce Cars-SRPlates, a novel dataset comprising 100,000 paired low- and high-resolution license plate images captured under real-world conditions. This dataset overcomes the limitations of existing resources that depend on synthetically degraded images, providing a more realistic benchmark for developing and evaluating super-resolution (SR) methods within the context of LPR. Our evaluation involves two SR models, Pixel-Level Network (PLNET) and Pixel-Level Network (LCDNet), alongside three fusion strategies - Highest Confidence (HC), Majority Vote (MV), and Majority Vote by Character position (MVCP) - to assess their impact on Global Perception License Plate Recognition (GP_LPR) performance. Results demonstrate that SR significantly improves recognition accuracy, especially when multiple super-resolved images are combined using majority-vote-based fusion techniques. Specifically, LCDNet paired with the MVCP strategy achieves the highest recognition rate, boosting accuracy from 38.2% to 44.2% using sequences of five images. These findings underscore the critical role of SR in enhancing LPR accuracy under adverse conditions.

Title: Toward Advancing License Plate Super-Resolution in Real-World Scenarios: A Dataset and Benchmark

Summary:

In summary, the main contributions of this work are:

  • A publicly available dataset named Cars-SRPlates containing 100,000 images, divided into 10,000 video tracks of cars, where each track contains five LR and five HR images of the same car license plate. 5,000 tracks were collected at a resolution of 1280×920, while the remaining 5,000 tracks were captured at 1920×1080. The dataset is balanced between Mercosur and Brazilian LPs layouts, across both resolutions.
  • Experiments conducted on the Cars-SRPlates dataset utilized the super-resolution models Pixel-Level Network (PLNET) [Nascimento et al., 2023] and Pixel-Level Network (LCDNet) [Nascimento et al., 2024]. For each track, five super-resolved images were generated from the low-resolution (LR) sequences. Inspired by [Laroca et al., 2023b], we explored the temporal characteristics of LR images by feeding the super-resolved images into the Global Perception License Plate Recognition (GP_LPR) model, employing three fusion strategies: sequence-level majority vote, character-level majority vote, and highest-confidence prediction. Notably, utilizing five super-resolved images with character-level majority voting resulted in an improvement in recognition accuracy.

How to Access the Dataset

The Cars-SRPlates dataset is available for academic research purposes and is free to researchers affiliated with educational or research institutions for non-commercial use. To request access, carefully review and complete the license agreement, then send it to the first author at vwnascimento@inf.ufpr.br. Please ensure the email is sent from a valid university domain (e.g., .edu, .ac, or similar). A download link will typically be provided within 1-5 business days. Incomplete requests or failure to follow the instructions may result in no response.
